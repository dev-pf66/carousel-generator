## Case Study #1: Borderless

### The headline
> "When traditional M&A breaks down, we build new ways to move value."

### Client
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Company | Borderless |
| Sector | Immigration & Global Mobility (B2B Skilled Worker Sponsor Licenses) |
| Geography | United Kingdom |
| Stage | Seed-funded, raised $2.5M in 2023 |
| Revenue at start | $2-3M ARR |
| Goal | Add £1M-£5M to Series A valuation — non-dilutive |

### The problem
- VC-backed companies command ~20× revenue multiples
- Acquiring £50K-£250K annualized cashflow could add £1M-£5M to enterprise value
- But the UK immigration law market wouldn't sell — full-firm acquisitions required long diligence, regulatory reviews, founder-dependent books
- Partial client-book sales viewed as risky by operators, reputationally sensitive
- **Key insight:** Borderless didn't need ownership — they needed access to cashflow clients

### The reframe
> "If sellers won't sell their clients, how else can cashflow be transferred — compliantly, quickly, and with trust?"

Pivot from acquisition → **referral-based overflow partnerships**. Referring firms send excess demand to Borderless for downstream economics.

### Execution numbers
| Input | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Engagement length | 3 months |
| Hours invested by Pocket Fund | ~960 hours (~80 hrs/week) |
| Advisors scraped (GOV.UK, Companies House, ILB) | ~1,500 |
| Targeted outbound campaigns | 300 |
| Social outreaches (LinkedIn, Facebook, WhatsApp) | ~600 |
| Direct cold calls | 200-250 |
| Serious conversations | 43+ |
| Strategically aligned operators | 8-10 |
| Client's equivalent UK labour cost saved | £11,500-£12,000 (at £12/hr min wage) |

### Outcome
- **£70K-£80K annualized cashflow** referral agreement in principle
- Final documentation pending at time of case study
- **Valuation uplift:** £50K-£250K in new ARR × ~20× VC multiple = £1M-£5M in additional enterprise value
- Scalable, repeatable partnership framework built

### Timeline
| Phase | Duration | Outcome |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Week 1-2: Market Mapping | 2 weeks | ~1,500 advisors from GOV.UK, Companies House, ILB, communities |
| Week 2-3: Outreach | 1+ month | 43+ serious conversations; 8-10 strategically aligned operators |
| Week 3-4: Qualification | Parallel | Strategic pivot from acquisitions to referral partnerships |
| Week 5-6: Structuring | < 6 weeks | Referral agreement in principle; £70K-£80K annual value; repeatable framework |

### Methodology framework (5 pillars)
These are Pocket Fund's named principles — useable as carousel frames:

1. **Market Exhaustion, Not Sampling** — "we evaluate addressable markets to ensure clients see every viable opportunity, not just the obvious ones"
2. **Problem Reframing Over Process Adherence** — "when traditional structures fail, we don't force them. We redesign the transaction to align with market realities"
3. **Intelligence as Outcome** — "every conversation — even rejections — becomes strategic market research"
4. **Optionality Over Forced Execution** — "we build pipelines that clients can activate on their timeline, not ours"
5. **Trust-First Execution in Relationship-Driven Markets** — "strategic communication and client identity to build credibility before pushing transactions"

### Reusable carousel angles
- "We were hired to buy a company. We built a partnership network instead. Here's why." (reframe story)
- "1,500 outreaches → 43 serious convos → 8 aligned operators → £1M+ valuation uplift" (funnel)

## Case Study #2: Dino Games

### The headline
> "Reallocating risk through payment timing instead of price compression."

### Deal snapshot
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Asset | Dino Games (9 mobile title portfolio) |
| Seller | Brownberry Games (20+ years ops experience across enterprise + SaaS) |
| Seller context | Just exited founder-led software business, carving out pre-revenue/unmonetized apps |
| Portfolio themes | Health, fitness, lifestyle, 2-min dinosaur sim |
| Sourcing | Off-market, via Pocket Fund's proprietary founder-led sourcing engine (Reddit + Twitter) — NOT App Store / Google Play scraping |
| Total consideration | $39,000 |
| Multiple | ~1.1× annual revenue, ~1.2× normalized annual profit |
| Structure | ~56% cash at close, ~44% deferred over 12 months (retention-linked) |
| Upfront payment | ~$22,000 |
| Payback — current performance | 7-8 months |
| Payback — target | 12-18 months |

### Asset fundamentals
- ~8M Android installs, sustained ~200K-300K monthly active users
- ~$3,000 monthly net profit on $3,000-$4,000 ad spend → ~40-50% net margins
- Entertainment-first audience (younger, short repeat sessions, low maintenance)
- Revenue skewed Tier-1 (US/UK) for high-CPM geos; Tier-2/3 markets support volume via puzzle/word games
- Eight-year operating history, two years without active updates

### The critical pattern (why this case matters)
**Seller was a sophisticated operator** with prior enterprise experience — rare in sub-$1M mobile apps.

Most seller-sourced deals:
- Founder-led, emotional, no exit strategy
- Short monetization history
- Need senior operator cleanup post-close
- Low buyer transparency

This deal:
- Exited operator with 20+ yrs ops experience
- Mature monetization stack (sub-10% D30, ~4% D7)
- Evidence-backed understanding of competitive dynamics
- Shared near-term assumptions with buyer
- Pricing conservative because value comes from **optimization, not turnaround**

### The negotiation move (Phase VII — Payment Re-Engineering)
> **Pocket Fund never publicly "renegotiates price."**

Instead, Pocket Fund pushed value into:
- Deferred payments
- Escrow-controlled releases
- Inspection contingencies
- Keeps seller economics conditional on **post-transfer reality**

**Effective result:** headline price preserved, buyer exposure reduced materially at close.

**Real example from the deal:** Pocket Fund requested seller turn OFF lifetime ($27) iOS purchases pre-close — avoided buyer inheriting perpetual obligation. Seller absorbed the lost revenue. Included as Telegram screenshot in the case study.

### The risk-reallocation framework
"By reallocating risk through payment timing instead of price compression, Pocket Fund delivered a market-consistent, capital-efficient acquisition of a proven, self-funding mobile gaming asset with clear, optimization-led upside and limited execution risk."

## Cross-case patterns (Pocket Fund's signature moves)

These show up in BOTH case studies — strongest material for "how we operate" carousels:

1. **Off-market, founder-led sourcing** — Borderless scraped advisors from public registries (GOV.UK, Companies House); Dino came from Reddit/Twitter. Neither came from standard marketplaces.
2. **Market exhaustion over sampling** — evaluate every addressable target (1,500 for Borderless; broad Reddit/Twitter sweep for Dino)
3. **Reframe the problem when execution stalls** — Borderless pivoted from acquisition → referral partnership; Dino pivoted from price negotiation → payment restructure
4. **Risk reallocation, not price compression** — headline terms preserved, exposure reduced via structure (deferred payments, escrow, retention triggers)
5. **Trust-first in relationship-driven markets** — both cases close via relationship quality, not just commercial terms
6. **Execute within real-world constraints** — "acquire cashflow, not complexity"
