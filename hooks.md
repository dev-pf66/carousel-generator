# Hook Library

A bank of proven hook patterns for LinkedIn carousel slide 1. Pull from here instead of inventing from scratch.

## How to use this file

1. When planning a carousel, identify the **primary engagement trigger** for the topic (curiosity, loss aversion, controversy, etc.)
2. Browse the matching section below, pick 2-3 candidate hooks
3. Adapt the template with specifics from the current topic (real numbers, real sectors, real pain)
4. **Always generate 3 hook variants** per carousel and let the user pick — never commit to one hook blindly

## Rules for any hook

- **First 2 lines only** — that's what shows in the LinkedIn feed before "...see more"
- **Specificity beats cleverness** — "80 deals, 2 closed" > "many deals, few closed"
- **No throat-clearing** — no "So I've been thinking..." or "Recently I noticed..."
- **Numbers in the hook** — specific numbers trigger authority and curiosity
- **Contrarian > consensus** — agreement doesn't get shared, disagreement does
- **Cut adjectives** — "huge mistake" → "mistake". Adjectives feel like padding.

## 0. PROVEN HOOKS — Dev's own top 6 LinkedIn posts (check this first)

Extracted from real posts with verified engagement (Mar 24 – Apr 20, 2026). These patterns already worked for Dev's exact audience — clone before inventing.

### 0.1 Stat-Pair Receipt (Post #1 · 4/3 · 5,509 imp · 1.89%)
Front-loads a shocking input stat + a shocking output stat across two lines. Zero setup.

| Template | Example (actual) |
|----------|-----------------|
| `I [action] a [thing] for [shocking low $]. It was [shocking low stat].` + `[Time window] later, [shocking high stat]. [Exit outcome].` | I bought a micro SaaS for $4,000. It was making $500/mo. / 6 months later, it was doing $5,000 MRR. I eventually exited for 6 figures. |

**Why it works:** Cash-on-cash arithmetic in line 1-2 creates instant authority + specificity. Readers can't scroll past a math problem they want solved.

### 0.2 Triple-Negation + One-Conviction (Post #2 · 4/15 · 4,535 imp · 2.05% · +43 followers)
Identity claim, then three "No X" lines, then "Just [minimum viable ingredients], and one conviction — [contrarian belief]."

| Template | Example (actual) |
|----------|-----------------|
| `I [did unexpected thing] with almost nothing and zero background in [field].` + `No [credential]. No [credential]. No [credential].` | I started a micro-HoldCo with almost nothing and zero background in M&A. / No MBA. No PE firm. No rich uncle with a fund. |

**Why it works:** The negation ladder is pure identity marketing — reader sees their own "nothing" reflected, feels permission. Best follower-conversion hook in the set.

### 0.3 System-Critique + Counter-Position (Post #3 · 3/25 · 3,339 imp · 2.25%)
Two-line contrarian stance. Name an establishment system in line 1, reject it in line 2.

| Template | Example (actual) |
|----------|-----------------|
| `[Dominant model] was built for [establishment persona].` + `[Your market] doesn't need that playbook.` | The US search fund model was built for Stanford MBAs. / India doesn't need that playbook. |

**Why it works:** Declares a tribe + an enemy in 10 seconds. Commenters pick sides instantly.

### 0.4 Myth / Setup / Negation (Post #4 · 4/4 · 3,208 imp · 2.06%)
Three-line hook: name the overcomplication, mock the expected solution, deliver the napkin.

| Template | Example (actual) |
|----------|-----------------|
| `Most people overcomplicate [topic].` + `They think you need [elaborate tools].` + `You don't. You need [simple metaphor].` | Most people overcomplicate business valuation. / They think you need a CFA, a DCF model, and 47 tabs in a spreadsheet. / You don't. You need a napkin. |

**Why it works:** Sets up a strawman then knocks it down with a concrete image ("napkin"). The image is the payload.

### 0.5 Headline + "Hot take:" Label (Post #5 · 3/23 · 1,189 imp · 3.87% ⭐ highest % in set)
Title-style headline (no verb) then an explicit "Hot take:" label, then a prediction.

| Template | Example (actual) |
|----------|-----------------|
| `[Headline — no verb, 5-8 words, nationally-framed]` + `Hot take: [prediction about your market].` | India Doesn't Need Another VC-Backed Startup / Hot take: India's next wave of wealth won't come from building unicorns. |

**Why it works:** The "Hot take:" label telegraphs "disagreement invited" and triggers comment-reply behavior. Nation-level framing adds identity-wedge energy. Highest engagement-rate pattern.

### When to use each

| If you want... | Use |
|----------------|-----|
| Max reach / algorithm push | 0.1 Stat-Pair |
| Followers, not just reach | 0.2 Triple-Negation |
| Audience-building in a niche/geography | 0.3 System-Critique |
| Tactical value post (save-heavy) | 0.4 Myth/Setup/Negation |
| Debate + comments + saves (tribe banger) | 0.5 Hot Take Label |

**Rule:** Before writing ANY new hook, check §0 first. Only move to §1-10 (generic patterns) if none of these 5 fit the topic.

## 1. Curiosity Gap — "You don't know what I know"

Optimizes for: swipes, saves, profile visits.

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| `Nobody tells you this about [topic].` | Nobody tells you this about seller financing. |
| `Here's what [authority] won't say about [topic].` | Here's what brokers won't say about LOI clauses. |
| `I wish someone had told me [specific thing] before my first [event].` | I wish someone had told me about working capital before my first close. |
| `The [topic] advice everyone gives is wrong. Here's what actually works.` | The deal sourcing advice everyone gives is wrong. Here's what actually works. |
| `The [N] things I learned in [timeframe] that no one talks about.` | The 7 things I learned doing 80 deals that no one talks about. |
| `After [specific reps], here's the one thing I keep seeing.` | After 80 acquisition calls, here's the one thing I keep seeing. |
| `There's a reason [surprising observation]. And it's not what you think.` | There's a reason most acquisitions fail in year one. And it's not what you think. |

## 2. Loss Aversion — "Stop doing this before it costs you"

Optimizes for: saves, shares (pain-avoidance).

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| `Stop doing [X] before it costs you [specific consequence].` | Stop signing LOIs in 7 days before it costs you the whole deal. |
| `[Specific mistake] almost cost me [specific number].` | One cap-table assumption almost cost me $200K. |
| `[N] red flags that cost me [amount]. Save this.` | 8 red flags that cost me $500K. Save this. |
| `The mistake that killed our last deal. Don't make it.` | The mistake that killed our last deal. Don't make it. |
| `I paid [amount] to learn [lesson]. So you don't have to.` | I paid $50K in legal fees to learn this. So you don't have to. |
| `If you're doing [X], you're about to lose [Y].` | If you're ignoring working capital in your LOI, you're about to lose 6 figures. |

## 5. Controversy / Hot Take — "You're wrong about this"

Optimizes for: comments, shares (debate).

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| `Unpopular opinion: [consensus belief] is wrong.` | Unpopular opinion: "Off-market deals" are overrated. |
| `[Popular belief] is a lie. Here's why.` | "Add value with no ask" is a lie. Here's why. |
| `Everyone's doing [X]. Everyone is wrong.` | Everyone's sourcing on BizBuySell. Everyone is wrong. |
| `Stop romanticizing [thing]. It's just [reality].` | Stop romanticizing search funds. It's just outbound + Excel. |

| `I'm going to say what no one will: [blunt truth].` | I'm going to say what no one will: most searchers are wasting two years. |

## Template: Generate 3 hook variants

When briefing a carousel, always produce this table before building:

```
| # | Hook (first 2 lines) | Trigger | Why this |
|---|---------------------|---------|----------|
| 1 | [variant 1]         | §N      | Best for [audience] because [reason] |
| 2 | [variant 2]         | §N      | Best for [audience] because [reason] |
| 3 | [variant 3]         | §N      | Best for [audience] because [reason] |
```

User picks. That hook becomes slide 1. No ambiguity.
